Moon Phases

Lunar Phases

The moon has eight total phases. These phases are the result of the changes in the amount of light visible on the moon's surface. The sun illuminates the moon's surface. These phases are the new moon, waxing crescent, first quarter, waxing gibbous, full moon, wanning gibbous, last quarter and wanning crescent.

New Moon

The moon first phase or Lunar Phase is called the new moon. This phase occurs when the sun and the moon have the same ecliptic longitude. This phase makes the moon look completely black because it is silhouetted against the sun (solar eclipse).

Full Moon

In this phase, the moon is fully illuminated in the night sky. During this phase the Earth is located between the the Sun and the Moon with the side of facing Earth completed illuminated by the sun.
